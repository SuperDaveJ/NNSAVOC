Office of Information Services

The Office of Information Services plans, directs, and oversees the delivery of centralized information technology (IT) infrastructure, applications, and information management (IM) services.Â  Its functions include:

  • Developing and implementing IT and IM plans, architecture, and policies
  • Ensuring agency IT and IM resources are selected and managed in a manner that maximizes their value
  • Directing and coordinating agency-wide information resources planning
  • Coordinating the development and annual update of OIS-related information
  • Directing the IT Capital Planning and Investment Control Process
  • Coordinating the activities of the IT Business Council
  • Providing office input on OIS plans, policy, and standards
  • Developing coordinated responses to Commission, OMB, and congressional inquiries
  • Coordinating activities associated with the NRC's participation in the Federal CIO Council
  • Coordinating the development and update of the agency-wide information resources management policy
  • Providing authoritative assistance, consultation, and guidance in the area of computer security and compliance
  • Providing advice and oversight to ensure the NRC complies with Best Practices and applicable Federal Laws and regulation
