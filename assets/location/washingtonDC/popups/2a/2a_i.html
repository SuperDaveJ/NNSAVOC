Civil Service Retirement System

The Civil Service Retirement System (CSRS) is a retirement system for certain Federal employees. It was replaced by the Federal Employees Retirement System (FERS) for Federal employees who first entered covered service on and after January 1, 1987.

The CSRS is a defined benefit, contributory retirement system.  Employees share in the expense of the annuities to which they become entitled. You may contribute 7%, 7.5% or 8% of your pay to CSRS.Â  The NNSA matches your CSRS contributions, percentage for percentage.
