Inspector General

The responsibilities of the office of the Inspector General (IG) include:

  • Provide leadership and policy direction in conducting audits and investigations designed to promote economy, efficiency, and effectiveness within the NRC.
  • Work to prevent and detect fraud, waste, abuse and mismanagement in agency programs and operations.
  • Recommend corrective actions to be taken, report on progress made in implementing those actions, and report criminal matters to the Department of Justice.
  • Analyze and comment on the impact of existing and proposed legislation and regulations on the economy and efficiency of NRC programs and operations.
  • Keep the Chairman and Congress informed of serious issues and concerns related to NRC programs and operations.
