Quick Fact – Workplace Safety Tips

  • Know where the nearest fire exits and stairwells are in relation to your office.
  • Know your escape route and assembly area in the event of an emergency.
  • Know where the first aid kit is in your office.
  • Maintain a tidy work environment, make sure equipment and materials are securely stored when not in use and egress pathways are clear.
  • Stay on defined walkways, walk do not run, look where you are going.
  • Do not text or talk on a cell phone while driving to and from work.
  • Cooperate with worker protection professionals conducting inspections and be informed of results of inspections and investigations.
  • Participate in required safety training including information regarding use of toxic materials in your workplace.
  • Maintain awareness of the conditions in your workplace and be prepared to stop work when these conditions pose an imminent danger of death or serious physical harm.
  • Immediately report unsafe or unhealthful working conditions to your supervisor. Employees have the right to report such concerns without fear of retribution.
  • Workers that believe they are being denied the rights or are being subjected to reprisals for attempting to exercise those rights may file an employee concern using the Department of Energy Employee Concerns Program.
